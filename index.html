About Us

Starting locally, Green Ways promotes environmentally friendly and sustainable ways that we can live, with the aim of conserving the planet for all inhabitants.

We run 'Waste Not', a Reading-based project to receive and recycle unwanted items. We want to reduce expenditure for all, to reduce emissions on production and unnecessary travel to shops, and to support community.

We aim to support and develop sustainable, low-carbon alternatives to the current consumerist and growth-based paradigm. We link our local environmental work to what is happening globally to our planet.

The 'Waste Not' Free Shop

Many items that we buy for ourselves, our home and our children are already available and unwanted in our communities. At the Green Ways Hub at 13a Southview Park, Caversham RG4 5AF, you can:

  • - find free items that you need. This can be anything; saucepans, books, wellies, coffee tables, clothing, birthday cards.

  • - donate unwanted items, including items that charity shops are not usually taking such as floorboards, pieces of insulation, all the items that end up in the bin or at the local tip. We take most things except sofas and white goods. If an item is large please email or DM us ahead to check that we have space for the item.

  • - can respond to and make requests for items from the community (eg I need a shelf bracket, dog bed, radiator key etc).

The household items that we gather are also redistributed through partnerships with local refugee organisations and charities supporting people affected by domestic violence. We are happy to take referrals.

As we develop further, we plan to create a local resource of items such as quality garden tools, carpet cleaners and other items that can be loaned - removing the need for people to buy and store their own equipment.

The statistics on waste and recycling in the UK highlight the urgent need to create a functioning system to share resources, reduce consumerism and live more sustainably:

  • - UK households throw away 155,000 tonnes of domestic electrical waste annually, hoarding nearly 20 small old electrical items per household. (Recycle-more.co.uk).

  • - The UK has the highest consumption of new clothing in Europe, with the average person buying 26.7kg of new clothes each year. The average lifetime for a piece of clothing is estimated to be 2.2 years leading to around 300,000 tonnes of used clothes either being burned or sent to landfills on an annual basis.

    • This initiative is a complement to, and is not intended to replace, valuable and successful established initiatives such as Freegle, Freecycle, the multiple Facebook Groups and Buy Nothing Networks etc.

    Green Ways uses several of these networks to offer items when we are running out of space in the Hub. Our own community research has shown that some people may struggle to access free items available online: they may have no transport/access to the internet, or cannot meet the deadline requested.

    When people are offering items, they may need the items gone as soon as possible.

    Charity shops are becoming more mindful of what they receive as the costs of disposal increase.

    The 'Waste Not' free shop is another way to acquire free items and to dispose of items that are no longer needed.
